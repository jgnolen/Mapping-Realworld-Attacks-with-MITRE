To address the hypothetical issue of insufficient security measures in Greenville Technical College's cybersecurity program, the following three methods could be implemented:

    Regular software updates and patch management:
    Keeping software up-to-date is crucial for maintaining a secure environment. Regularly updating and patching operating systems, applications, and security software can help protect the organization from known vulnerabilities that hackers might exploit. Greenville Technical College should establish a routine patch management process to ensure that all systems are updated promptly, reducing the risk of security breaches.

    Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA):
    By requiring users to provide at least two forms of identification before granting access to sensitive systems or data, multi-factor authentication can significantly reduce the risk of unauthorized access. MFA typically involves a combination of something the user knows (e.g., a password), something the user has (e.g., a hardware token or a smartphone), and/or something the user is (e.g., a fingerprint or facial recognition). Implementing MFA across the college's network, particularly for privileged accounts and remote access, can strengthen overall security.

    Regular employee training and awareness programs:
    Human error is often a significant factor in security breaches. Providing regular training and awareness programs for faculty, staff, and students can help create a culture of cybersecurity awareness. These programs should cover topics like recognizing phishing emails, creating strong passwords, securing personal devices, and understanding the importance of reporting suspicious activity. By empowering users with knowledge and best practices, Greenville Technical College can create a more robust first line of defense against potential threats.

Implementing these three methods can help address the issue of insufficient security measures and improve Greenville Technical College's cybersecurity program, reducing the risk of future security breaches.
